About ICCMASD 2026

International Conference on Contemporary Management Approaches to Sustainable Development-2026 (ICCMASD-2026) is being organized by the Department of Management Studies, SMIT in hybrid mode at Majitar, East Sikkim, India during 25-26 Sep, 2026.

Management becomes a significant interdisciplinary segment that finds its utility in almost all areas of Business and Social Sciences. The fast-changing face of Business Management has further raised its importance and placed a great demand on its application to real-life situations and problems. The aim of ICCMASD-2026 is to accumulate and exchange innovative ideas, latest knowledge and unique experiences for the benefit of researchers, industrialists, academicians and esteemed delegates. In the age of globalization and privatization, various issues in the field of Business Management arise resulting in integration of the systems, functionalities and business operations. The proposed events hall makes an honest endeavor in the aforesaid direction to foster an outcome-oriented approach in the defined fields. It will have an interface amongst the professionals from industry and academia to address key issues in modern business management and the challenges faced by the business world in the ongoing pandemic era

Publication Information

All papers (after due review) received for ICCMASD-2026 will be accepted for Conference Presentation. Abstracts of the accepted/presented papers will be published with Proceedings with ISBN.

Select Full Papers may be published (optional) in Scopus Indexed Journals as per guidelines, terms, and conditions of the affiliated Journals. The publication process in this regard will take a minimum of 4–8 months from the ending day of the event.

Submission Guidelines

Paper Submission Guidelines

All submissions must be written in English and must be made in Microsoft Word Format (.doc/.docx) using Times New Roman, font size 12, and line spacing 1.5.

Full Paper Structure (4000–5000 words)
  • Title Page: Paper title, author(s), affiliation, postal address, phone number, and email.
  • Abstract: 150–200 words covering objectives, scope, methods, results, and conclusions.
  • Keywords: Maximum 4–5 words.
  • Introduction: Background and motivation of the study.
  • Objectives: Key goals of the research.
  • Theory (if any): Relevant theoretical framework.
  • Literature Review: Comparison with existing research.
  • Methods: Research design, methodology, and analysis.
  • Results: Findings (use tables/figures where possible).
  • Discussion: Interpretation and comparison with existing knowledge.
  • References: APA 6.0 format, alphabetical order, single-spaced with hanging indent.
Evaluation Criteria

Papers will be evaluated based on methodological rigor, relevance of the study, clarity of communication, appropriateness of results and discussion, and overall contribution.

Additional Notes
  • Future work, implementation details, and proofs may be included if relevant.
  • Similarity Index must be 10% or below (excluding references).
  • All papers must be submitted through the conference website.

How to Reach Sikkim

Sikkim is one of the seven states in North East India, nestled in the lap of the Himalayas. Known for its scenic beauty, rich biodiversity, and vibrant culture, it is home to Kanchenjunga, the world’s third-highest peak. The state offers convenient transportation options, making it accessible for travelers from across the country and beyond.

✈️ By Flight

The nearest airport is Bagdogra Airport (approximately 124 km), well connected to major cities in India. Airlines such as Air India, Indigo, Air Asia, and SpiceJet operate regular flights. From Bagdogra, you can hire a taxi (approx. 3 hours drive) to Gangtok. Helicopter services are also available, subject to weather conditions.

🚆 By Train

The nearest railway stations are New Jalpaiguri (188 km) and Siliguri (145 km), both well connected to major cities across India. From there, you can take a bus or hire a taxi to reach Gangtok and other parts of Sikkim.

🛣️ By Road

Road travel is one of the most convenient ways to reach Sikkim. Frequent public and private bus services connect Sikkim with nearby towns and cities. Taxis and shared jeeps are easily available for inter- and intra-state travel. National Highway 31-A is the primary route connecting Sikkim. Nearby destinations include Darjeeling, Kalimpong, Dooars, and Siliguri.

About the Region & Institution

About Sikkim

Sikkim is a landlocked Himalayan state bordered by Nepal, Bhutan, Tibet, and West Bengal. Once a British protectorate, it became a part of India in 1975. Despite being the least populous state, Sikkim is geographically diverse, with climates ranging from subtropical to alpine regions.

Home to Kanchenjunga, the world’s third-highest peak, the state is globally recognized for its breathtaking landscapes, rich biodiversity, and vibrant culture, making it a major tourism destination.

About Sikkim Manipal University (SMU)

Established in 1995 under an Act of the Government of Sikkim, SMU is recognized by the UGC under Section 2(f). It is part of the prestigious Manipal Group, known for excellence in education and healthcare globally.

SMU operates on a Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model and has been accredited by NAAC. The university is committed to delivering quality education, especially to students from North and North-Eastern India.

About SMIT

Sikkim Manipal Institute of Technology (SMIT), established in 1997, is a constituent college of SMU and approved by AICTE. It has received NAAC accreditation and is certified under ISO standards, reflecting its commitment to academic excellence.

Located at Majitar near Rangpo, the campus lies along the Teesta River, surrounded by lush green hills, providing a safe and inspiring environment for academic and personal growth.

Department of Management Studies, SMIT

The Department of Management Studies at SMIT is committed to excellence in management education by nurturing skilled professionals capable of addressing global challenges with integrity and innovation.

The department offers BBA, MBA, and PhD programs designed to prepare future leaders through a multidisciplinary approach. Regular seminars, workshops, and industry interactions ensure practical exposure and holistic development.
